Cars for the future!

Electric cars are the future of the auto industry

Automotive companies are preparing for a future in which sales of electric vehicles will be higher than sales of vehicles with internal combustion engines.

Another automotive company confirmed that the future of the auto industry belongs to electric vehicles - after Volvo recently announced that from 2019 all new models will have an electric or hybrid engine, now Huandai have announced a "change in production strategy" whose center will be in the future electric vehicles. The company's plans in the next few years include 3 plug-in hybrids and eight electric cars and construction the first factory for the production of exclusively electric vehicles that will allow them to develop a larger number of e-vehicles with a higher range.

The next year, we can expect an electric version of the model SUV Kona, whose range with one battery charge is almost 400 km, and Hyundai's plans include the development of an electric limousine under its Genesis brand, which should be on the market in 2021 with a range of around 500 km which will compete with Tesla, but Robin Zhu, an automotive market analyst for the BBC, said that in the next four years we can expect further progress of Tesla batteries, and then the range of their cars will be even greater than today.

Otherwise, Hyundai last year introduced its first fully electric car intended for the mass market - IONIQ, but as for most similar e-vehicles, its problem is a battery that is weaker than the cars from Tesla and General Motors and the range with one charge amounts about 280 km.

In a few years, we can expect that almost all automotive companies will have several models of electric and hybrid vehicles in offer - their price will then probably be more competitive (especially if countries continue to grant subsidies and privileges to their customers). Until then infrastructure should not be a problem as the number of charging stations will increase with increasing vehicle sales, while charging should be faster.

According to data released by the International Energy Agency, last year over 750 thousand electric cars have been sold. Even though their share in relation to classic petrol and diesel engines is the highest in Norway, when it comes to specific figures, most cars were sold in China, which has occupied more than 40 percent of the global market.
